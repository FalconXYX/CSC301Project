- Improves the use of Scrum through coaching, facilitation and rapid elimination of any distractions to the team  
- Ensure principles behind scrum remain intact even as the companyâ€™s own implementation evolves

**Product Owner**  
- Managing ROI (Return On Investment)  
- Establishes a shared vision for the product among the customers / developers  
- Knowing what to build and in what order  
- Owns the product backlog  
- Creating release plans and establishing delivery dates  
- Supporting spring planning and reviews  
- Representing the customers  

**Developer and domain experts**  
- Members of the team are primarily developers and testers, however in many projects, specialists from other disciplines may also join the team.

## Decision making:
- Heavily rely on user stories from the product backlog for goal setting.

- The team will use a points grading scheme for the level of difficulty of implementing new user stories. During the scrum meetings when a new user story is added to the product backlog, the team will collectively grade the story with a certain number of points (relative to the other stories) depending on how hard the team conceives this user story to be. This is so that the user stories can be broken down into smaller implementable tasks that can be split among the team if that user story is too much for only one person to complete.

- The **sprint length** will not vary in time as they are due at certain dates. Though the team can, and should, plan to have intermediate made-up sprint deadlines that will help us desolve any larger tasks into smaller ones. This should be decided by the scrum master.
